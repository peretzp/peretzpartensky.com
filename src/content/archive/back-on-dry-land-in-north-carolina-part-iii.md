The author completed a 1,000-mile sailing journey along the U.S. East Coast, departing from New Bern, North Carolina and concluding in Atlanta before returning to San Francisco. The voyage involved stepping up Borka's masts, navigating canals and bridges, staying in marinas, and ultimately having the vessel dry-docked for hurricane season.

Throughout the journey, the boat functioned as "an extension of our bodies," creating a floating home requiring constant attention to mooring procedures, wind conditions, and navigational coordinates.

From the water, the crew observed nuclear power facilities including Indian Point on the Hudson River, Hope Creek and Salem Reactors on the Delaware River, and Calvert Cliffs on the Chesapeake Bay. They also encountered Navy vessels including a blimp and the USS Ike aircraft carrier.

Sailing at night on the open ocean proved exhilarating, with stars serving as navigation references. The experience resembled wandering Burning Man's playa, with other vessels appearing like elaborate art cars against the darkness.

The most memorable encounter involved Henning Bohm, a 45-year-old Danish sailor who had been sailing solo for decades. After receiving a terminal diagnosis with less than 25% survival probability, Henning purchased a boat to sail westward. His journey encountered multiple catastrophes: autopilot failure, a heater fire, steering wheel damage, and ultimately Hurricane Alberto near Cape Hatteras. During the hurricane, he described "a blindfolded ride on a roller coaster" lasting over 12 hours, during which waves flooded both the cabin and cockpit. He survived by sprawling in the cockpit, bruising extensively while attempting to steer blindly into invisible winds.

After the hurricane, Henning retreated northward to Hampton, Maryland, where he hand-pumped 288 buckets of water from the boat. Subsequently running aground while attempting a safer intracoastal route, he required Coast Guard assistance a second time and hospitalization for internal bleeding.

When the author's crew met Henning at Alligator River Marina, he had been recovering for two weeks. Despite his ordeal, the crew invited him to dinner and assisted with sail repairs.
