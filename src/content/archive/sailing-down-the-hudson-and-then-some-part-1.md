For the next few weeks, the author will sail down the East Coast, beginning at Lake Champlain and navigating through 20 locks to reach the Hudson River. The journey continues with mast placement near New York City, followed by sailing through the harbor, into Chesapeake Bay, and along the intra-coastal waterway as time permits.

The vessel is a "sloop rigged catch"—a double-masted ship with a single front jib. It features an unusual "bilge keel" configuration with two keels extending from the sides. This design offers stability when the tide recedes, keeping the boat upright rather than allowing it to tip over.

The boat, a Westerly 33" Ketch named "Borka," is captained by Tim Nulty. The crew includes Louisa Bukiet, Lindsay (visiting from England), and the author.

The post concludes by recommending two sailing narratives: Tim Anderson's account of "To Cuba (almost) by Outrigger Sailing Canoe" and Moxie Marlinspike's "Hypothermia / Brink of Death," both describing notable maritime adventures and challenges.
