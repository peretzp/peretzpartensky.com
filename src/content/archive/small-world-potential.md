When you unexpectedly encounter someone you know, it feels like "a small world." However, the author argues this reveals something different: your social world is actually small because you've exhausted your "small world potential" by circulating within the same social circle.

Each new person you meet expands this potential by creating future reconnection possibilities. The author suggests that with practice, one can accumulate this potential to "propel oneself into stable orbit" within expanded social networks. Rather than maintaining a fixed home base, a person with high small world potential could live nomadically, continuously meeting new people and creating interconnections without wearing out welcomes.

The concept crystallized when describing a particular person who embodies this capacity—someone now staying with the author for a month, demonstrating the practical manifestation of high small world potential.
