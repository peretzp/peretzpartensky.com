The author reflects on the philosophy that "life is practice for life," extending this principle to billiards strategy. They suggest taking the second easiest shot in billiards as practice for future games, with a humorous caveat that this approach only applies when young. The author adds an emphatic warning that this strategy becomes "extremely inapplicable" when facing one's final game ever, emphasizing the absurdity of treating a last moment as mere practice.
