On the way from Lake Champlain to New York City, the author navigated a series of 12 locks comprising part of the NY State Canal System. The author reflects on the historic significance of these canals, particularly the Erie Canal, which connected New York harbor to the Great Lakes and Chicago, facilitating western expansion and establishing New York City as the nation's economic center.

The canals represented massive engineering achievements. Today they serve primarily historical, symbolic, and recreational purposes. Maintenance costs approximately $100 million annually while generating only $200,000 in toll revenue yearly. During the author's passage through 11 locks on the Champlain Canal, they were the sole vessel, yet their transit displaced over 15 million gallons of water.

The journey provided historical education, passing Fort Ticonderoga, site of an early Revolutionary War victory led by Ethan Allen in 1775, Bannerman's Island Arsenal, and West Point Military Academy.

A crew member, Lindsey Annison from Appleby, Cumbria, England, joined the adventure after attending a fiber optics conference. The author highlights her remarkable accomplishments: she tweets with her MP Rory Stewart, knows creators of the "Extreme Shepherding" YouTube video, and has experienced superfetation—bearing twin daughters conceived a month apart.
