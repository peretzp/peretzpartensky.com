… or My Sunday Skiing Accident

The author describes suffering a severe skiing accident that resulted in two large hematomas on his rear end after launching off a jump without knowing the terrain on the other side. He estimates approximately fifteen feet separated his takeoff from landing.

The Fall:
During the descent, the skier encountered an unexpected abrupt drop. He attempted protective measures by crouching, positioning ski poles as a cavity over his elbows, and trying to absorb impact with bent knees. His skis and poles flew away upon impact with a rock, and he tumbled down the slope. Upon regaining consciousness in the snow, he found himself able to move his fingers and toes despite significant trauma.

Damage:
Ski patrol transported him via snowmobile to the medical clinic. Two massive bruises emerged—one on his left buttock resembling a pomegranate, another on his right thigh like an eggplant. The doctor indicated these represented some of the worst hematomas he'd observed and would likely require draining within days. The author received Vicodin and a prescription for pain management.

Treatment and Recovery:
For forty-eight hours, he used ice-filled ziplock bags secured with binder clips while sleeping elevated. He employed ergonomic seating to distribute weight toward his knees while writing.

Lessons Learned:
The author concludes by retiring from mountain sports, recognizing the pattern of repeated injuries. He decides to restrict participation to activities with better risk-to-reward ratios while maintaining his appreciation for challenging pursuits in safer contexts.
