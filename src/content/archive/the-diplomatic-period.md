For a period, the author signed electronic communications with "_./peretz_" as a personal reference to work at an open source nonprofit in Portland. The signature also cleverly referenced the Linux command line prefix "./" used to execute non-executable files in directories with execution privileges.

Due to a habit of regularly phasing out non-essential practices, the author discontinued this signature style. Recently, upon receiving an official document titled "attestation en anglais," the author noticed it ended with "_./._" and promptly alerted authorities about what appeared to be a typo.

The secretary to the French ambassador to Nepal responded with an intriguing revelation: "The './.' at the end is called 'the diplomatic point.'" Though Google cannot confirm this terminology exists, the author found the explanation perfectly logical—that diplomats might employ this command line technique to mark executive correspondence, much as the author had intended with their personal signature.
