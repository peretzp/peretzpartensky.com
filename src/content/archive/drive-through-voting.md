As voter turnout increased, the author notes that casting a ballot remains inconvenient and could benefit from streamlined processes.

The proposal suggests leveraging existing banking infrastructure. Financial institutions could be mandated to provide voting services upon customer request, utilizing uploaded voter registration information to transform voting into a straightforward ATM transaction. Banks already possess the identity verification and data management capabilities necessary for such a system.

A more ambitious suggestion involves fast-food drive-through windows. While acknowledging that McDonald's lines occasionally rival those at polling stations, the author humorously notes that at least drive-through customers receive "fries with that." This tongue-in-cheek approach to higher voter participation highlights how convenience might meaningfully increase electoral engagement.
