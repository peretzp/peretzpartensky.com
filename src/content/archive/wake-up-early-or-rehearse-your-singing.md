The author describes receiving a schedule for their first day in China that includes an unusual incentive for punctuality. After arriving from a 15-hour flight, participants are expected to meet at 7:40 am, with a creative consequence: "The latest one would be invited to sing a song on the way."

The author reflects on this arrangement as both clever and amusing. The public nature of the penalty serves as a collective deterrent against tardiness while increasing individual alertness for chronic oversleepers. As someone who identifies as an oversleeper without a singing voice, the author reframes this potential punishment as an opportunity—a chance for "institutionally controlled practice" for future karaoke experiences while maintaining plausible deniability about participating voluntarily.

The post concludes with a request for song suggestions appropriate for the occasion. The author also notes personal intentions to write explanatory content about the context of their China trip and the broader goals of their blog and personal mission.
