Shailendra Kumar Upadhyay (born April 17, 1929) died climbing Everest on May 9, 2011. As he liked to say, he was "82 years young".

The author first met Shailendra in Nepal during summer 2008 in Kathmandu. By conventional standards he was already elderly, yet remained actively engaged in his country's political affairs during a turbulent period following the king's deposition and the Maoists' transition into government. Shailendra served as an intermediary between opposing factions.

His remarkable career spanned both rebellion and governance. He participated in Gandhi's independence movement in India and was imprisoned in Nepal (1960) for anti-monarchic views. Later, he represented Nepal at the United Nations (1972-1978), served as ambassador to various countries, and held the position of Minister of Foreign Affairs (1986-1990).

In 2008, Shailendra mentioned dreaming of climbing Mount Everest. He casually remarked about wanting to die there, then laughed. Over subsequent years, his training intensified. He aimed to become the oldest person and first octogenarian summiting the world's tallest mountain.

During long hikes, Shailendra entertained companions with stories from his eventful life. As a former Soviet refugee, the author found his accounts of fundraising from the Soviet Communist Party for Nepal's communist faction particularly intriguing.

He died pursuing his mountaineering ambition.
