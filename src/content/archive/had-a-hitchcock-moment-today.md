While working at his desk, the author was startled by a large pigeon smashing into his window. The bird was in an unnatural flight position, surrounded by its own feathers, before regaining altitude and returning to the trees safely.

This incident reminded him of another shocking moment from summer 2002, the day before his 21st birthday, when a bird rammed its beak into his head without provocation while he was peacefully strolling along Chicago's waterfront with a friend named Kris.

The author notes that severe wind has been present for three days. During an occasional nighttime walk through Cambridge while leaning into a strong gust of wind, he caught a fleeting scent of the sea—an unexpected sensory moment brought by the weather.
