Immigrants in Europe demonstrate entrepreneurial spirit by circumventing minor legal restrictions for profit. In Barcelona, liquor stores close at 10pm and bar beer costs over 3 euros, yet street vendors deliver cans for 1 euro or less through negotiation. At Milan's train terminal, restroom access costs 0.80 euros, but immigrant operators have rigged the gate to allow entry for 0.20 euros. A 12-year-old enforces payment; those refusing face aggressive consequences from others in the group. Police occasionally raid these operations, but everyone denies involvement, and life continues unchanged.
