The author attended a MathOverflow dinner at the Joint Mathematics Meeting where he unexpectedly encountered Donald Knuth in the restaurant lobby. When asked if Knuth was there for the MathOverflow dinner, the legendary computer scientist confirmed he had read about it online but wondered if the event was actually happening.

During their conversation, Knuth expressed surprise at the author's iPhone, asking what the device was. The author demonstrated the touch interface and messaging capabilities. Knuth responded that he might acquire such technology "when I am ready to communicate again," noting he was maintaining freedom from distractions while writing his magnum opus. He humorously suggested that in twenty years, when his work might be complete, such gadgets could become so intricate he'd never understand them.

Knuth shared a personal story about his lifelong fascination with algorithms and Al-Khwarizmi, the mathematical figure from whom the term "algorithm" etymologically derives. In the 1970s, when Al-Khwarizmi's birthplace appeared unreachable in Soviet Uzbekistan, Knuth mentioned this regret to a Russian Academy colleague. Two years later, he received an official invitation to an algorithms conference in Khiva. The pilgrimage succeeded spectacularly, complete with ceremonial welcomes. The group dedicated a town square statue to Al-Khwarizmi, which Knuth later confirmed still stands via satellite imagery.

The evening concluded with an intimate celebration of Knuth's 72nd birthday, marked with cake and song.
