The author attended traffic court and observed approximately 30 cases before their own. Most defendants pleaded guilty or no contest to multiple charges. About 40% were no-shows with bench warrants issued; some faced $15,000 bail as flight risks.

Common infractions included marijuana possession ($158 fine, the "only misdemeanor for which the California legislature actually bans jail time"), speeding, running stop signs, illegal turns, and driving without insurance. Speeding violations qualified for traffic school at an additional $24 cost, which most defendants accepted.

One notable case involved a tractor-trailer driver charged with an illegal U-turn ($178) and failing to keep a logbook ($540). When questioned about accident severity, the driver stated the incident was fatal. Despite this, the judge found the report attributed responsibility to the other driver, seeming puzzled by the minor charges given the fatal outcome.

The author's own case involved pleading not guilty and requesting a trial by written declaration, which was granted with a two-month continuance for discovery. The author was notably the first defendant to plead not guilty in the observed proceedings.
