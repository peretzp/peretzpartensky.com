The author describes an encounter with a Russian punk in Barcelona who explained the significance of shoelace colors within punk culture. According to this punk, "the black shoelace means life sucks. that is the main theme of the punks. the other is red because i am half ready to go to battle."

The punk elaborated that two red laces indicate immediate readiness for fighting. He also noted that "neo-nazis wear white laces. when punks wear white laces, they are stepping on the neo-nazi terrain."

The group the author met included a young female punk with red dreads who approached asking for cannabis (which she called "cho-co-latte"). They had been living in a Barcelona squat for a couple of years, sustaining themselves through discarded restaurant food and soliciting for drugs.

The punk's cigarette case served as a visual metaphor for their philosophy: a collection of different cigarettes from various sources, representing their "collect enough rocks and you will have a mountain" approach to survival.

The author notes that videos of these individuals and their pit bull are available online.
