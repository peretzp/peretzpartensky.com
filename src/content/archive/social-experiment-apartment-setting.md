The author recalls an old politically incorrect Russian joke about Chinese naming practices, initially dismissing it as culturally insensitive. However, a curious incident prompts reconsideration.

Upon dropping a spoon in his room, the author's Chinese flatmate John (who uses an Americanized name due to pronunciation difficulties) suddenly calls out "yes" from down the hall, seemingly responding to the noise. Intrigued by this coincidence, the author decides to conduct a follow-up experiment.

Three hours later, with a friend present as witness, the author accidentally clanks his cup on the table while John is in the kitchen. John again responds with "yes" and walks to the door to check if someone is there.

The author concludes that John likely has sensitive hearing and receives quiet knocking from timid friends—a characteristic the author confirms John actually possesses. The experiment humorously tests whether the stereotypical joke might contain unexpected truth.
