The author describes attending an official opening ceremony at China's Ministry of Science and Technology early in the morning. The group of participants was escorted into a formal conference hall where they were seated with individual microphones at each table, served tea throughout the presentation.

Six officials representing various Chinese and American institutions and the U.S. embassy took positions at the podium. The author notes that the Chinese representatives "are remarkably good at self-organization" during a group photo, in contrast to the American participants.

Each panelist delivered speeches emphasizing institutional collaborations and used phrases like "You are the ones that will light the torch of science and technology collaboration" and references to overcoming challenges from the Sichuan earthquake.

A presenter named Mr. Li then delivered an extensive 100-slide PowerPoint presentation titled "Introduction to China: Science and Technology Development." The author humorously imagines the design philosophy behind such presentations: fill every slide with logos, images, graphs, tables, and dense bullet points while keeping text small.

The presentation covered statistics on science investment, five-year plan achievements, and China's efforts to move up the production value chain. After the final slide, attendees were dismissed and returned to their hotel by 11:30 AM, leaving the author feeling "delightfully unrewarding and sobering."
