The author receives a call from his friend Crazy Alex, a fellow alumnus from a Boston liberal arts college and Russian compatriot who has been living in Moscow for three months. Alex announces he's returning to America with a ticket for April 27th on United Airlines.

Alex asks the author to call him back on Skype, then describes a violent incident he witnessed while walking in Moscow. He recounts observing homeless individuals being attacked by street youth with a beer bottle. When Alex confronted the attackers using a decorative katana sword he had purchased, the perpetrators fled.

Alex reflects that this experience reinforced why he wants to leave Russia. He shares the story with his friend Nastya, who expresses envy of his American passport and ability to "exact civic justice" before departing.

The author concludes with observations about taking action in the present moment. He notes that Alex, now entering the San Francisco job market, has experience in finance but prefers theater and occasional freelance work.

The post captures a dramatic moment that motivated Alex's decision to return to America, illustrating the broader tensions between his adopted and native countries.
