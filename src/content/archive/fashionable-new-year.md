For New Year's celebrations, the author traveled to Milan, described as "the fashion capital of the world," to report on current trends.

The festivities spanned three plazas, each featuring differently themed concerts, with interconnecting streets offering various entertainment options.

The author humorously describes dangerous activities occurring in the plazas, presented as fashionable pursuits: throwing firecrackers into crowds and aiming projectile fireworks at attendees to maximize shock value. For added sophistication, some participants placed firecrackers in beer bottles before throwing them to cause injury from glass shards, with ambulances conveniently stationed nearby.

The post notes that cheap champagne costs approximately five euros from street vendors. Breaking bottles after consumption, particularly just after midnight, is described as fashionable behavior.

The author concludes that while these activities are trendy themselves, performing them with style matters—neighbors notice, though they reportedly won't contact authorities.
