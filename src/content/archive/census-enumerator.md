In April, the author accepted a temporary census position and completed training in May at a community facility near China Basin. Though colleagues suggested the work seemed beneath someone with a PhD in Biology, the author viewed it as an opportunity to study humanity while enjoying outdoor exercise and neighborhood exploration during pleasant weather.

The author's assignment focused on Single Room Occupancy (SRO) hotels near 6th and Mission Street, which housed vulnerable populations including recovering addicts, parolees, and individuals with mental health challenges. These facilities differed dramatically from better-maintained SROs serving elderly or working families—residents of the author's assigned buildings often remained isolated in their rooms.

The enumeration work revealed striking human encounters. One resident questioned why they should be counted, claiming invisibility. Another individual required repeated introductions due to amnesia, while a schizophrenic person interpreted the occupancy question literally regarding voices in his head. An elderly gentleman enthusiastically traced his lineage to Spanish royalty before disclosing suspicions about hidden bank accounts.

The author documented encounters ranging from genuine cooperation to outright hostility. One resident threatened violence before a woman emerged from the bathroom to counsel him about social expectations and parental responsibilities. The author also observed the informal economy within SROs: residents living on approximately five dollars daily through SSI benefits engaged in bartering cigarettes, cookies, and services.

Beyond the buildings, street-level scenes emerged—an elderly man striking soda cans with a cane while an elderly woman watched adoringly, describing the activity as "fun." Management staff, predominantly Indian and Nepali, operated as gatekeepers providing census information and cultural insights.

The author reflects on refusing cigarette bribes during training, though sympathetic engagement represented its own form of persuasion. The experience concluded with uncertainty about the deeper implications of these encounters occurring mere blocks from home.
