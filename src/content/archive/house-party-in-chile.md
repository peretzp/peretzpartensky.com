A friend named Felipe invited the author to a house party in Chile that began at 2AM. The gathering celebrated the birthdays of brothers aged 22-24, with attendees filling every available space. Empty Pisco and Coke bottles and tracked mud covered the house and backyard, while loud music played throughout.

What surprised the author most was the parents' presence. The father greeted guests upon arrival while the mother socialized in the kitchen with friends. When noise levels became excessive, the father would emerge to lower the stereo volume or close open doors, then retreat without apparent frustration.

The author mentioned attending similar parties in the United States, typically occurring when parents were away. The birthday host seemed confused by this approach. When asked why he wouldn't throw such a party in his parents' absence, he replied seriously: "Then who would clean up?"
