---
title: "The Machine Is the Context"
date: 2026-02-27
description: "On building personal infrastructure that outlasts any single conversation."
---

The conversation window is ephemeral. The filesystem is the real memory.

Every interaction must produce durable artifacts — code, config, protocols, logs, API endpoints — that future instances can read and continue from. You never "run out of context" because you externalize state to the right layer.

This is the founding principle of the personal operating system I've been building: treat your data as architecture, not as content. Your workflows are not one-off requests — they are features of a living system.

When a pattern recurs, codify it. When a tool is useful, extract it. Interactions become infrastructure.
